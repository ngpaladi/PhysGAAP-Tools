We are a group of current MIT Physics graduate students working to reduce the barriers for application to our physics PhD program and to encourage students from traditionally underrepresented backgrounds to apply. We provide several resources to assist prospective applicants throughout the graduate school application process, as part of the MIT Physics Graduate Application Assistance Program (PhysGAAP). We offer webinars (the first one to be held on September 29, 10:00am EDT, register here (https://tinyurl.com/physgaapwebinars21), a mentoring program (applications open on September 29), and a Q&A service (ongoing).

We hope you can forward this email and share the information in this Google Doc (https://tinyurl.com/MITPhysicsAppResources) with physics undergraduates at your university who are interested in applying to graduate school, and we hope you particularly encourage students from historically excluded communities to do so. Even if your students are not planning on applying to graduate school during this cycle, please encourage them to attend the webinars to find out more about what graduate school and the application process is like. Thank you in advance for your assistance!
